Pashupatinath Temple

About

Pashupatinath Temple is one of the most sacred Hindu temples in Nepal, located on the banks of the Bagmati River in eastern Kathmandu. The temple is dedicated to Pashupati, an incarnation of the Hindu god Shiva.

History

The temple was built in the 5th century and later renovated by Malla kings. The existing temple was constructed in the 17th century to replace the previous one which was destroyed by termites.

Significance

Pashupatinath is the most important temple dedicated to god Shiva. Every year, thousands of pilgrims from all over the world visit this temple.

Visiting Hours

4:00 AM to 9:00 PM daily

Entry Fee

NPR 1000 for foreigners, free for Nepali citizens

Pashupatinath Temple

Address

Pashupati Nath Road, Kathmandu 44600, Nepal

Directions

Located approximately 5 km northeast of Kathmandu and is easily accessible by taxi or bus from the city center.

Nearby Places

- Boudhanath Stupa (3 km)
- Guhyeshwari Temple (0.5 km)
- Kathmandu Durbar Square (4 km)
